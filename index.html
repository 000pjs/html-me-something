Fig's Theme

"Don't say you're sorry... " Fig narrows her eyes at me as she cracks 2 eggs into the frying pan. "Don't. ..start with ME! -- (lit with euphoric candor) -- Go downstairs and grab the laundry and then grab the water jug from the car." I sigh and move toward the backdoor, quiet and happy, placing my hand on her shoulder for a moment, then ruffling the ears of a greying 100 lb dog. She's waiting to be fed, her name is Barbie. The smell of broccoli, eggs and rice fill the small apartment. As I head downstairs to street level, big, fat raindrops are plodding on the windows, sounding like a steel factory and it's cool. I stand in the backdoor-way watching the trees sway.

Just then I hear a man scream in anger. "Fuuuuccckk!!!" It's loud enough for the entire block to hear. I peak around a brick building to the alleyway, and I see a young down-and-out buzz cut man rifling thru the trash in front of a neighboring unit. He's screming his lungs out. Before I can register a black sedan pulls up. Two men get out, bag his head and stuff him inside. As they pull off, a woman is reavealed and she starts walking toward me. She flashes a concerend smile and says, "You didn't see anything this evening." And I say "I didn't see anything this evening," as I turn around and return to the apartment.

3 days have passed and I wake from a an unexpected nap, and it's mid-afternoon. Eyes still blurry and fat with allergy tears, bringing streaks of light in through the window like a wash of watercolors -- yellows, blues and greens. Old gingko-looking leaves are shifting and dancing together perfectly. Fig and Barbie are asleep next to me, twitching with the R.E.M.s. Light pours in and the room evapoates.
